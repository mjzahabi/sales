Overview

What is it, exactly?

Think of it as a knowledgeable colleague sitting beside you who can read whatever's on your screen. The extension brings Claude into a persistent sidebar in Chrome — aware of the page you're on, ready to help you think, write, summarize, or act on it instantly.


It integrates with your Claude.ai account, so your conversations, Projects, and memory carry over seamlessly.

⊞
Persistent sidebar Opens alongside any webpage without replacing what you're reading
◎
Page-aware context Claude can read the current page when you choose to share it
⟳
Connected to Claude.ai Your Projects, history, and preferences come with you
🔒 Privacy: Claude only processes page content when you actively share it. It does not monitor your browsing history or read pages in the background.
Setup

Getting started in three steps.

01

Install the Extension

Visit the Chrome Web Store and click Add to Chrome. Takes under 30 seconds. The Claude icon will appear in your toolbar.


→ Open Chrome Web Store
02

Open the Sidebar

Click the Claude icon in your toolbar to open the sidebar. It stays open as you move between tabs. You can also set a keyboard shortcut in Chrome's extension settings for even faster access.

03

Share a Page & Start

Navigate to any webpage you want help with, then use the option in the sidebar to share the current page with Claude. From there, just ask — it already has context.

Use Cases

Six ways your team can use it.

Each scenario below represents a real workflow where the extension saves time and reduces the mental load of switching tools.

Use Case 01

Summarizing Long Web Pages

Turn walls of text into clear takeaways in seconds.

Whether it's a lengthy industry report, a dense product changelog, or a competitor's blog post, reading everything in full is rarely efficient. The extension lets you pull out what matters — like having a research assistant who's already done the skimming for you.

How to use it
  • Open the article or report in your browser
  • Open the Claude sidebar
  • Share the current page with Claude
  • Ask Claude to summarize the key points or pull specific information
Example scenario
Reviewing a vendor's "What's New" page
  • You're on a vendor's release notes page with dozens of feature updates
  • You share the page and ask Claude a targeted question
  • Claude returns a focused, relevant summary — not a full recap
  • You paste it into your Slack update in 2 minutes instead of 20
Summarize the top 5 changes most relevant to our CRM workflow
💡 Pro Tip: Pair with a follow-up like "What questions should I be asking this vendor about these changes?" to get even more value from the same content.
Use Case 02

Write professional responses without leaving the page that prompted them.

You're reading a customer complaint, a stakeholder's feedback form, or a competitor's announcement and you need to respond. Instead of switching to email, writing blind, and switching back — keep it all in one place. The extension is like having a draft already waiting when you're ready to reply.

How to use it
  • Navigate to the content you need to respond to
  • Share the page content with Claude
  • Ask Claude to draft a message with the context already included
  • Review, lightly edit, and send
Example scenario
Responding to a product feedback thread
  • You're reading a user feedback thread about a missing feature
  • You share the thread with Claude in the sidebar
  • Claude drafts a professional, on-tone reply with the specifics incorporated
  • Your response is accurate and specific — not generic
Draft a response that acknowledges their feedback and explains our roadmap direction

How to use it
  • Open any webpage with relevant research content
  • Share the page with Claude

  • Forward Claude's synthesis as a competitive brief or summary
Example scenario
Evaluating a competitor's feature list

Prompting Tips

Get more out of every prompt.

Tip 01

Be specific about your audience

"Summarize this" gets a generic output. "Summarize this for a VP who has 2 minutes and no technical background" gets something immediately useful. Naming the reader changes everything.

Tip 02

Tell Claude your role in the situation

"I'm a product manager preparing for a prioritization meeting" or "I'm a customer success rep writing to an upset client" gives Claude the context it needs to tailor output to your actual situation.

Tip 03

Iterate rather than accept the first draft

The first response is a starting point. Follow up with "make it shorter," "add more warmth," or "what did you leave out?" The sidebar is a conversation, not a vending machine.

Tip 04

Use it for thinking, not just writing

"What should I be worried about with this approach?" or "What am I missing here?" can surface blind spots before you act on incomplete information. Claude isn't just a writing tool.
