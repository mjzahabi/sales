◆ Decision 01

W-2 Employees or 1099 Contractors?

This decision reshapes every other choice — payroll obligations, tax filings, compliance burden, and annual cost. Start here.

Do you control when, where, and how the work gets done?

↓
Yes → W-2 Employee

You set schedules, methods, and provide tools

If workers are in Discord during market hours following your playbook, they're employees. You handle withholding, FICA, SUI, workers' comp, and quarterly filings. More overhead, but more control and less audit risk.

No → 1099 Contractor

You define deliverables, they choose how

If workers deliver finished content on their own schedule with their own tools and serve other clients, they're contractors. You file a 1099-NEC once a year. Simpler, but you can't dictate the process.

W-2 Route

Full employee compliance
  • Withhold federal & MO state income tax each pay period
  • Pay employer FICA (7.65%), FUTA (0.6%), MO SUI (~2.51%)
  • File Form 941 quarterly, 940 annually, W-2s by Jan 31
  • Carry workers' comp insurance
  • Track shareholder-employee salary for reasonable comp
  • More control over schedules, methods, and team integration
~$55K / worker / year at $50K salary (incl. employer taxes)

1099 Route

Contractor simplicity
  • No withholding — contractors handle their own taxes
  • No employer FICA, FUTA, SUI, or workers' comp
  • File 1099-NEC by Jan 31 for each contractor paid $600+
  • Collect W-9 from each contractor before first payment
  • Document independence with written contractor agreements
  • Less control — cannot dictate hours or methods
~$50K / contractor / year at $50K flat (no added taxes)

⚠ Misclassification Risk

Missouri uses the IRS 20-factor test. If the IRS reclassifies contractors as employees, Jake faces 100% of the employee's unpaid FICA + employer share, penalties up to $50 per unfiled W-2, plus compounding interest going back 3+ years. A contract alone doesn't protect you — actual practices must match the paperwork.

💡 First-Year Strategy

Consider spending ~$600 with a CPA for year one to set the foundation — establish reasonable salary documentation, confirm basis tracking, and ensure Missouri MO-1120S is filed correctly. Then take that return as a template and switch to TaxAct or TurboTax for subsequent years. Think of it as paying for the playbook once, then running it yourself.
