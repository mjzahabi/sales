What is this extension?

The PI Sales Intelligence Extension is a Chrome browser tool that lives on top of VinSolutions. It captures a dealer's Predictive Insights data automatically and uses it to instantly populate four professional sales presentation documents — all from a single click.

Think of it as your personal backstage crew. Before the extension existed, loading a dealer presentation meant navigating to the Automotive Intelligence dashboard, manually reading off numbers, entering them one by one into a separate document, and hoping you didn't transpose anything. That process took 15-20 minutes and introduced human error into the most important data you'd show a dealer.

The extension eliminates all of that. It sits quietly in the background whenever you're on a VinSolutions page. The moment you're on the Automotive Intelligence dashboard, it reads every buying signal metric directly from the page. When you click a button in the sidebar to launch a presentation document, your data is already there — dealer name, market-level signals, source breakdowns, previously sold counts, sales comparisons — all live and accurate, all ready to present.

How it works — the two-step model

The extension has one job: read the dealer's Predictive Insights data from the dashboard and carry it into a presentation document without any manual steps. Here's exactly how that happens.

→
3
Click a document button
Choose one of four presentation documents from the sidebar
→
4
Document opens pre-populated
The dealer's actual data is already inside. Present immediately.

What data is captured automatically? When you're on the Automotive Intelligence Dashboard, the extension reads: total customers with buying signals, active leads with buying signals, market-level signal counts (Browsing / Actively Shopping / Ready to Buy), source breakdown counts (Autotrader, KBB, Dealer.com, Accelerate My Deal), previously sold customer counts, retention vs. acquisition figures, and the sales comparison month-over-month data. It also pulls the dealer name from the page context.

What requires manual entry? A small handful of fields aren't on the PI dashboard and must be entered manually: your name and contact details (for the presentation footer), and the dealer's total database size and mailable customer count, which come from the Campaigns tab. The sidebar makes these fields easy to find and edit before launching any document.

Important — be on the main dashboard, not a drill-down

Automatic data capture works on the Automotive Intelligence main dashboard (the one with the summary tiles and market-level cards). If you're on a drill-down report like Buying Signal Details or Sentiment Analysis, the extension will still show the sidebar but won't capture data from those pages. Navigate back to the main dashboard first, then let the extension capture, then drill into sub-reports if needed.

The PI Dashboard Presentation

The flagship document. A full narrative presentation that walks a dealer through the invisible customer journey, the Cox data moat, their actual buying signal metrics, and the cost of doing nothing. This is the primary tool for Predictive Insights discovery conversations.

🎯
PI Dashboard Presentation
Full Predictive Insights story — data moat, dual journey roadmaps, buying signals dashboard, retention vs. acquisition
Launch Button: 🎯 PI Dashboard Presentation
When to use it
Best situations
Discovery meetings focused on Predictive Insights, competitive churn risk conversations, any meeting where you need to explain "why Cox data is different," or when a dealer is considering canceling VinSolutions and you need to demonstrate the value of the integrated ecosystem.
Audience fit
General Manager, Dealer Principal, or Internet/BDC Director. Anyone who controls the CRM vendor decision. This presentation is designed to shift how they think about customer intelligence — from reactive (lead management) to proactive (buying signal-based outreach).
Narrative structure — walk the dealer through this sequence
1
The 84% headline
Open with the stat: 84% of the customer journey happens before a lead is submitted. Let it land. Ask the dealer: "Do you know what your customer is doing for the first five weeks before they fill out a form on your website?" Most won't have an answer — that's exactly the point.
2
The blind spot narrative
Walk them week by week. The customer is on KBB in week 1, Autotrader in weeks 2-3, their Dealer.com website in weeks 4-5, and the lead (maybe) comes in week 6. Let them feel the gap between what they can see and what's actually happening.
3
The data moat
Point to the source signal counts — these are their real numbers, not hypotheticals. Explain that first-party data from Autotrader, KBB, and Dealer.com is data no other CRM vendor can access. Then direct their attention to the ecosystem stat strip above the source cards.
The numbers that end the debate

When a dealer asks "why is your data different?" or a competitor claims similar intelligence, these are the stats that end it: Cox Automotive processes 2.7 billion online visits, 24 billion consumer interactions, 12 billion ad impressions, 180 million service records, 30 million credit applications, and 12 million sales records. No third-party data provider, no competitor CRM, no standalone marketing tool has access to this. It's not close. When the dealer sees those numbers sitting directly above their own signal counts from Autotrader, KBB, and Dealer.com, the value proposition stops being theoretical.

4
Why competitors can't replicate this
Four cards address the specific angles of competitive differentiation: exclusive data access, cross-platform identity, real-time signals, intent prioritization. Don't read them aloud — let the dealer scan. The 17M Autotrader visitors and 27M KBB users stats land hardest. If a competitor claims similar capabilities, ask: "Do they have access to Autotrader and KBB browsing data?" The answer is always no.
5
The dual roadmaps
This is the aha moment. Two timelines side by side: the left shows the customer lost at week 4 to a competitor. The right shows Predictive Insights intercepting at week 1-2 and closing by week 3. Ask: "Which outcome do you want?"
6
"Was that your customer?"
Let this question breathe for three to four seconds. Don't fill the silence. Then scroll to the Buying Signals Dashboard — which is now populated with their real data. Every number you show them is a real customer they might be losing right now.
7
The market-level cards and panel stings
Walk the five market-level cards left to right: Not in Market → Browsing → Actively Shopping → Ready to Buy. Each card shows the specific behavioral signals the AI measures — not guesswork, real browsing patterns. The Ready to Buy card has the killer stat: 9x more likely to purchase within 30 days. Let that land, then point to the Ready to Buy sub-metrics in the dashboard below. The italic sting lines under each panel frame the cost of inaction — let the dealer read those themselves.
8
Retention vs. acquisition — close here
The $700 vs. $70 comparison. Point to their Previously Sold Customers count and ask: "How many of these customers are going to buy from a competitor this month because nobody reached out?" Then pause. That's your close.
Common objections for this document
Objection
"Our CRM already shows us leads."
Your CRM shows you customers who raised their hand. But the customer who's been on Autotrader for three weeks and never submitted a lead is invisible to your CRM — and that's the majority of in-market shoppers. Predictive Insights shows you the 84% of the journey your CRM can't see.
Objection
"Another vendor told us they have similar data."
Ask them directly: "Does that vendor own Autotrader? Do they own KBB? Do they have 2.7 billion annual site visits?" The data moat isn't a feature — it's a property of who Cox Automotive is. No one else has it because no one else built these properties over the past 25 years.
Presentation tips
Don't skip the narrative

It's tempting to jump straight to the dashboard numbers. Don't. The story flow — blind spot → data moat → dual roadmaps → "was that your customer?" → their data — builds the emotional context that makes the numbers hit. Without the story, the numbers are just numbers.

Use dark mode for screen presentations

Dark mode looks more premium on screen and reduces glare in meeting rooms. Use the small toggle in the upper-right corner of the document to switch. Light mode works better if the dealer wants to print or screenshot anything.

When to use it
Best situations
Follow-up meetings after an initial PI discovery conversation, when a dealer wants to understand how PI and AMP work together, or when you need a comprehensive leave-behind document that a dealer can review and share internally with ownership or their marketing team.
Audience fit
Marketing Director, GM, or Dealer Principal who's evaluating the full Cox marketing ecosystem. This document answers "what do we do with the buying signal data?" — the answer is AMP, which turns signals into targeted campaign reach.
What the document covers — section by section
1
The headline number
Opens with the dealer's total "shopping for a car" count from the extension capture. The framing: their database already contains the next sales — the question is whether they have the tools to see and act on them.
2
Database stats and market segmentation
Total database size, the portion actively shopping, and real-time online activity in the last 72 hours. This section establishes the size of the opportunity — not industry estimates, but their actual database.
3
How PI and AMP work together
PI identifies who is in-market. AMP reaches them with targeted messaging before they submit a lead. The two-product story: identification without activation is just information, and activation without identification is just noise. Together, they're a precision strike.

Recommended presenting approach

Don't present all ten pages live — the document is designed as a hybrid: present pages 1-5 in the meeting (the story and ROI), then hand off or email the full PDF so they can review the signal breakdown on their own time. "I want to show you something. This is your dealership's buying signal profile — not industry data, not estimates, your actual customers right now. Key objections for this document
Objection
"We already do email marketing."
That's great — but are you targeting the right customers at the right time? Without Predictive Insights, you're sending the same message to someone just starting to browse and someone who's Ready to Buy today. AMP + PI lets you treat them differently. The Ready-to-Buy customer gets urgency. The browser gets nurture. Right message, right person, right moment.
Objection
"We don't have budget for new tools."
Point to the "Shopping — No Leads" number in the Priority Signals section. That's customers actively in-market who your team isn't talking to. If even 10% of them buy from a competitor this month, calculate the lost gross. The question isn't whether you can afford this — it's whether you can afford to keep not acting on data you're already sitting on.

The Connected Service Drive Presentation

A targeted presentation for the conversation that often gets overlooked: the service drive as a vehicle acquisition channel. This document shows how Xtime, vAuto, VinSolutions, and Predictive Insights connect to turn a routine oil change appointment into a sourcing and sales opportunity.

🔧
Connected Service Drive
Service drive acquisition story — sourcing cost comparison, iceberg blind spot, ecosystem flow, revenue impact calculator
Launch Button: 🔧 Connected Service Drive
When to use it
Best situations
Meetings with Fixed Ops Directors, Used Car Managers, or GMs who are frustrated with auction costs, dealers who don't use Xtime but could benefit from the connected workflow, or any conversation where the hook is vehicle sourcing rather than CRM intelligence.
The core premise
The average dealer spends $1,800 per unit to source from auction. A vehicle in their service drive costs roughly $350. The service drive customer already trusts the dealership, the vehicle was just on their lift, and Cox data already knows they're back in market. The only thing missing is visibility — and that's exactly what this presentation solves.
Before the meeting — preparation
The most important prep step for this document

Get the dealer's monthly RO count, their current used vehicle sourcing split (auction vs. trade-in vs. service drive), and their monthly used vehicle target before you walk in. The revenue impact calculator in the document adjusts in real time — if you can enter their actual numbers live in the meeting, the presentation stops being a pitch and becomes a custom analysis. Every field update changes the waterfall chart instantly.

Narrative structure — the 8-stop walkthrough
1
Open with a question, not a feature
Don't open the tool first. Start with: "If I asked you where your most profitable used car inventory comes from, what would you say?" Most dealers will say trade-ins or auction. Let them answer, then: "What if I told you your service drive is your lowest-cost, highest-margin acquisition channel — and most of those opportunities are completely invisible right now?" Then open the presentation.
2
The sourcing problem — anchor on cost
Walk the three source cards: auction, walk-in trades, service drive and lease returns. Point to the per-unit acquisition cost under each card. If the dealer questions the auction number, open the sidebar and update it live with their number. This is a powerful moment — they see their own data changing the entire analysis. Then: "If we could shift even 15% of your volume from auction to your service drive, that's [savings amount] per month in acquisition costs alone."
3
The blind spot — transition from cost to visibility
Point to the four stat cards: monthly appointments → total acquisition pool → identified today → identified with Cox. The contrast between what they're currently catching (5% organically, through chance conversations) and what the data shows is in market (15-18%) is the emotional hook. Ask: "How many of your service customers do you currently identify as potential trade or purchase opportunities each month?"
4
The iceberg — make the invisible visible
This is the visual aha moment. "Think of every service appointment like an iceberg. Your team sees the oil change on the surface. But beneath the waterline, Cox's ecosystem reveals a complete behavioral profile." Walk through each brand logo in the iceberg: KBB trade valuation, Autotrader browsing, Dealertrack lease maturity, Predictive Insights Ready to Buy score. "Your service advisor has no idea." Then ask: "How would it change your process if your salesperson knew all of that before the customer even checked in?"
5
The PI data section — show their numbers
If you launched from the extension, the PI data is already here. If not, the defaults still tell a compelling story, but frame them as "typical for a dealership your size." Walk the market levels, then point to the "Customers with no active lead" number. That's the one that stings. Then move to the Previously Sold panel and ask: "How many of these customers are going to buy from a competitor this month because nobody reached out?"
6
The ecosystem — keep this section brief
The animated data flow shows how Xtime → vAuto → VinSolutions → PI connect. Walk it once, name each brand as you point to it, but don't linger. The ecosystem is the mechanism, not the headline. The dealer cares about results, which come next.
7
The revenue calculator — let them change the numbers
This is where the presentation becomes a conversation. Open the sidebar if you haven't already. Walk through the waterfall: monthly appointments → back-in-market pool → identified without Cox → identified with Cox. Point to the gap. If the dealer questions any number, lower it immediately and show that the math still works at conservative assumptions. Then ask: "Does that gap surprise you? What would it mean for your store if you could identify even half of those additional opportunities?"
8
Competitive positioning — save for last
Never lead with competitors. After the dealer has bought into the data story, walk the side-by-side comparison: what CDK, Reynolds, Tekion, and myKaarma see vs. what Cox sees. "They operate within your four walls. Cox sees the 18 billion valuation lookups and 2.3 billion online visits that reveal what your customers are doing before, during, and after they walk through your door." Keep it factual, not disparaging.
Closing the conversation

"Based on what we've seen today, where would you want to start: getting your team visibility into Predictive Insights buying signals so you know which service customers are back in market before they arrive, or connecting your Xtime appointments to vAuto's Global Search so your used car team can act on those acquisition opportunities automatically?"

Objection
"We already do this with our DMS."
Your DMS sees the appointment. But does it know that customer browsed F-150s on Autotrader for three weeks, checked their trade value on KBB twice, and Predictive Insights scores them Ready to Buy? The DMS manages the transaction. Cox identifies the opportunity before the transaction exists.
Objection
"We don't have time for another tool."
This isn't another tool your team has to log into. The buying signals surface inside VinSolutions CRM, where your team already works. The Xtime-to-vAuto connection runs automatically. We're adding visibility to your existing workflow, not adding a new one.
Objection

Don't defend — adjust. "These are starting points. Let me drop the back-in-market rate to 12% — does the math still work for you?" Lower the number live in the sidebar. When the business case survives conservative assumptions, you've won the credibility battle.
Objection
"We're happy with our auction sourcing."
I'm not saying stop going to auction. What if 15-20% of your used inventory came from your own service drive at $350 per unit instead of $1,800? That's not replacing auction — that's diversifying your supply chain and improving your margin on every unit you shift.

Tips for maximum impact

Always use their data

A presentation that says "Heritage Toyota" at the top and shows their actual numbers is 10x more impactful than a generic demo with placeholder data. Launch from the extension, capture the PI dashboard, and enter the dealer name and contact info before you walk into the room. That 5 minutes of prep is the difference between a pitch and a conversation.

Pick the right document for the room

The 2-Page Report is a quick gut-punch. The PI Dashboard Presentation is the full story. The 10-Page AMP Analysis is the leave-behind business case. The Connected Service Drive is the acquisition and fixed ops conversation. You don't need to use all four in every meeting — match the document to the audience and time available. They all pull from the same captured data, so switching between them is a single click.

Let silence do work for you

After a big number lands — 9x more likely to buy, the "no active lead" count, the retention vs. acquisition cost gap — don't fill the silence with explanation. Let the dealer sit with it. The pause is not dead air; it's processing time. The best closes in this toolkit come after a three-second silence, not after a rush of talking points.

Adjust numbers live to build credibility

If a dealer pushes back on any calculator input, change it immediately and let them watch the analysis update. Lowering a number on the spot signals confidence — you're not attached to the defaults because the business case works at conservative assumptions too. Rigidity kills deals. Flexibility builds trust.

Use dark mode for screen presentations

Every document supports a dark/light theme toggle in the upper-right corner. Dark mode looks more premium on screen and reduces glare in meeting rooms. Switch to light mode if the dealer wants to print, screenshot, or read on their own device.

Close the sidebar before you present

The sidebar is your backstage prep area. Make sure it's collapsed before you share your screen or turn the laptop toward the dealer. The presentation should feel polished and complete — not like you're still configuring it.

Send the PDF the same day

Use the Save PDF or GoFullPage capture option to generate a personalized PDF and email it within two hours of the meeting while the conversation is still fresh. A dealer who has their own numbers in a document they can share with their team or their ownership group is far more likely to move the conversation forward than one who just saw a screen for 45 minutes.

Don't skip the narrative sections

It's tempting to jump straight to the dealer's data. Don't. The story that precedes the numbers — the blind spot, the data moat, the invisible journey — is what gives the numbers emotional weight. Without the story, you're showing a dealer a spreadsheet. With the story, you're showing them what they're missing.
