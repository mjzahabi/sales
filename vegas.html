🌮 Tacos El Gordo
3041 S Las Vegas Blvd — north of Wynn/Encore
Take the Deuce north (or walk 25 min) to one of the best taco spots in Vegas. Tijuana-style, counter-service, no frills. The adobada (spiced pork carved off the spit) is the #1 seller for a reason. Each station has a separate line — get in the adobada line first, then carne asada. Budget 20–30 min for lines. Cheap, fast, and outrageously good.
Lunch
Sphere photo op: The Sphere is right nearby (behind the Venetian). You can see its massive LED exterior from the street. Worth a quick walk-by for photos — no ticket needed to admire the outside.

📸 Welcome to Las Vegas Sign
5100 Las Vegas Blvd S — Deuce stop at south end of Strip
Take the Deuce southbound to the Welcome to Las Vegas Sign — it's one of the first stops. Quick photo op, dedicated parking area, usually 10–15 min wait in line. There are often friendly locals helping snap photos. Then hop back on the northbound Deuce and ride the entire length of the Strip — it's a sightseeing tour in itself.

1:00 PM
🎰 Fremont Street Experience
Downtown Las Vegas — Deuce stop: Fremont St
Ride the Deuce all the way to downtown. Fremont Street is old-school Vegas — neon signs, street performers, and the massive Viva Vision LED canopy overhead. Explore the Container Park (open-air shopping in recycled shipping containers), snap a free photo with a million dollars in cash at Binion's, and wander through the classic casinos. The SlotZilla zipline runs right over the street if you're feeling adventurous.
Free

Walk south from Fremont to the Arts District. Browse galleries, murals, and eclectic shops. Grab a drink at one of the trendy bars. This neighborhood has a completely different vibe from the Strip — more Brooklyn than Bellagio.

🎭 First Friday
18b Arts District — Downtown Las Vegas
April 3rd IS the first Friday of the month — lucky you. This is a massive free block party with live music, art installations, food vendors, and galleries open late. Thousands of people come out. Adults only (21+). No outside glass or bottles — grab drinks from the vendors. This will be one of the most memorable evenings of the trip.
Free Event

Fremont Street Experience
