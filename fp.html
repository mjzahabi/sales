What is Fullpath?
Fullpath is the automotive industry's most complete AI-powered data ecosystem for dealerships — think of it as the central nervous system connecting a dealer's fragmented data sources, activating that data across every marketing and sales channel, and increasingly automating the work through AI agents. It's not just a point solution; it's designed to be the operating layer beneath everything a modern dealership does.
Core Philosophy
One unified AI ecosystem. Not a feature. A platform.
Most automotive technology vendors solve one problem — a CRM here, an ad tool there. Fullpath was built from the ground up to be agnostic and connective: ingest all the data, unify it into a single shopper truth, then activate that truth across email, SMS, digital advertising, website personalization, and now an agentic CRM. The result is a platform that compounds in value the longer a dealer uses it, much like how a flywheel builds speed over time — each data source added makes every activation smarter.

What Makes Fullpath's CDP Different
Most CDPs in other industries are data warehouses — great for building reports, terrible for taking action. Fullpath built the activation layer directly into the CDP. Audiences built in the CDP immediately power ad campaigns, email automations, website personalizations, and AI agent workflows. There's no "export to another tool" step. This integration is the core differentiator.
Generic CDPs like Segment or mParticle can technically work for dealerships, but they require massive custom development. Fullpath was designed specifically for automotive workflows — it understands equity, VINs, service appointments, DMS data structures, OEM incentive feeds, and trade cycles natively. This vertical specificity means faster time to value and far fewer integration headaches.
Fullpath has deanonymized 10.9 million website shopper identities — meaning it can match anonymous website visitors back to known shopper profiles with high confidence. This turns passive website traffic into an active opportunity list. Most competing platforms can only act on known contacts; Fullpath acts on the invisible majority who browse without converting.
Fullpath holds ISO 27001 certification — the top international standard for information security. This is critical for dealers navigating FTC Safeguards Rule compliance and for enterprise-level buyers who require documented security postures. It's not just a checkbox; it's a demonstrated commitment to data governance that directly addresses dealer risk.

Financial Trigger Marketing
Equity Campaigns
Equity is one of automotive retail's most powerful but underutilized data signals — knowing that a customer has significant positive equity in their current vehicle is one of the strongest indicators of near-term trade readiness. Fullpath's Equity module ingests equity data into the CDP and uses it to trigger precisely timed email, SMS, and ad campaigns reaching those shoppers at their peak receptivity window.
Equity Threshold Triggers
Campaigns automatically fire when a shopper crosses a defined equity threshold — positive equity, negative equity, or specific dollar amounts — without manual list pulls.
Lease Maturity Integration

Personalized Offers

Multi-Channel Delivery

Offer Automation
Dynamic Payments
Dynamic Payments solves a pervasive and costly problem in automotive advertising: stale offers. When OEM incentives change, interest rates shift, or a vehicle gets repriced, the ads running across a dealer's digital channels often don't update for days or weeks — leading to shopper frustration, compliance risk, and wasted ad spend. Fullpath's Dynamic Payments module automates the synchronization of real-time payment information across all active campaigns and placements.
Real-Time Rate Sync
Connects to OEM incentive feeds, the dealer's DMS, and lender rate data to keep advertised payments current across all platforms without manual updates.
Cross-Platform Distribution
Updated payment information pushes simultaneously to Google ads, social ads, email campaigns, and website payment widgets — no channel falls behind.
Compliance Protection
Eliminates the risk of advertising outdated payments — a source of compliance exposure under FTC advertising standards and OEM co-op guidelines.

Fullpath was founded by three Israeli technologists — Aharon Horwitz, Yishai Goldstein, and Eliav Moshe — with deep backgrounds in data infrastructure, search technology, and product development. The company has a dual-hub model with engineering and product concentrated in Jerusalem and Tel Aviv, and commercial operations distributed across Vermont, Detroit, New Jersey, Salt Lake City, and remote staff throughout North America.
CEO & Co-Founder
Aharon Horwitz
The commercial and strategic voice of Fullpath. Known as an automotive AI thought leader, Horwitz speaks extensively at industry conferences and co-authored "The Last Dealership," a book analyzing how AI and automation will reshape automotive retail. He has shaped Fullpath's positioning as a platform company — not just a feature — and will be a critical voice in the integration narrative post-acquisition.
CTO & Co-Founder
Yishai Goldstein
The engineering backbone of the platform. Goldstein's background in search technologies, natural language processing, and data warehousing from Answers.com directly informs Fullpath's approach to identity resolution and large-scale data processing. Holds a BSc in Computer Engineering from Hebrew University. His work on the platform's security and ISO 27001 compliance is part of Fullpath's enterprise-grade credibility.
CPO & Co-Founder
Eliav Moshe
The product and analytics mind. A published author and experienced mathematician, Moshe has built Fullpath's analytics data layer from the ground up. His background in mobile app development (including #1-ranked apps), conversion optimization, and Google Analytics implementations gives Fullpath's product a strong data-first, user-behavior orientation — visible in how the CDP surfaces insights and audience recommendations proactively.

OmniAI initiative announced — Fullpath's autonomous AI suite. Lead Handling Agent released for configuration: custom agent persona/name, business hours, downtime behavior, escalation rules. Handles inbound leads 24/7 without salesperson intervention. Configurable via "Design Your Agent" settings.
Jan 2026

Fullpath announces automotive's first Agentic CRM — a multi-agent system handling real-time shopper intelligence, proactive task management, and automated lead engagement. Described as completing their "comprehensive AI ecosystem for car dealers."
2026

Cox Automotive acquires Fullpath, joining the Retail Solutions portfolio alongside VinSolutions, Xtime, Dealertrack, vAuto, and PI Sales Intelligence. MSS team begins transition to product ownership and presentation.

Source 1
CEO Interview — Aharon Horwitz  September 2025
The Consolidation Thesis — Direct Cox Threat (Pre-Acquisition)
"Take ~20 vendors down to one"
Horwitz's stated platform goal, verbatim from the CI memo. His public thesis was platform consolidation across front-of-house workflows — reducing the number of vendors a dealer buys from, at the expense of point-solution providers. CI noted this overlaps directly with categories where Cox operates: lead/marketing automation, merchandising, CRM-adjacent workflows, and inventory/retail enablement. Post-acquisition, this is now Cox's consolidation story to tell.
Growth Target
📈
20–30% U.S. Market Share in 5 Years
Horwitz's stated 5-year goal, up from approximately 7% market share at the time of the interview. He described the end-state as a system that "operates the dealership on behalf of the people that work there" — humans shifting to an approval-only role while agents execute. This ambition is now inside Cox.
Market Claim
🇺🇸
"A quarter of U.S. car sales" Touched by Fullpath
Horwitz's own estimate — CI flagged it as unvalidated externally and framed as his personal assessment. Still, the scale claim reflects the platform's breadth across dealers, OEM programs, and data integrations. CI treated it with appropriate skepticism in the memo.
Operational Reality
🇮🇱
Built for Resilience Around Israel Reserve Duty
Horwitz specifically cited redundancy in the engineering org to handle Israel reserve duty rotations and geopolitical disruptions. CI noted this as a real operational consideration — the engineering team is primarily Israel-based, and the company has consciously built staffing buffers around this. Worth understanding as a people/continuity risk.
Source 2
Platform Demo + Client Feedback  October 2024
Key Finding
🔗
VinSolutions Deep Link Already Live
When a shopper submits a lead, Fullpath routes it to the VinSolutions lead address and embeds a deep link in the lead record — giving salespeople access to the full Fullpath customer profile (website activity timeline, VDP views, equity position, loyalty score, marketing engagement) without needing a separate Fullpath login. This is a purposeful integration that puts Fullpath's intelligence directly inside the Cox CRM workflow. It's soft adoption — and it's already running in the market.
Key Finding
⚠️
CRM Encroachment Was Predictable in 2024
The October 2024 CI memo documented a client expectation that Fullpath would "release a CRM in the next 6–12 months." The Agentic CRM launched in January 2026 — right on that schedule. CI identified the pattern: Fullpath had already built campaign management, customer communication, and lead nurturing into the platform — functions traditionally owned by the CRM. The Agentic CRM was the formalization of what was already happening.
Key Finding
🏢
Enterprise Gap — Cross-Rooftop Records Didn't Transfer
As of October 2024, a customer record in one rooftop did not carry to another rooftop in the same dealer group. This prevented cross-store ad suppression, inventory search, and unified customer management. CI flagged this as a constraint on enterprise group sales — likely limiting Fullpath to single points or small groups at the time. The Group CDP product was the response, but worth validating how far this has matured post-acquisition.
Cox Callout
🎯
Cox AMP Called Out by Name as Lagging
CI noted directly: Cox's Automotive Marketing Platform "lacks AI integrations and automated workflows that would meaningfully reduce salespeople's task work and improve efficiency" — and that this gap allows Fullpath to lead in customer management and marketing despite not owning first-party data sources the way Cox does. This is the exact gap the acquisition is designed to close.
"Speed of innovation is a primary driver of Fullpath client satisfaction and provides a distinct advantage over legacy players in the lead management and marketing space. Other providers can offer similar functionality — particularly in terms of data integrations and some level of automation — but they have already fallen behind Fullpath in terms of development pace and connectivity between solutions."
— CAI Competitive Intelligence, October 2024 Demo Memo (client verbatim)
Source 3
Client Follow-Up Conversation  November 2024
aM Risk
⚡
automotiveMastermind Displaced — Live Example
A confirmed client had paused all automotiveMastermind equity mining and email campaigns in favor of Fullpath's offering. The reason: Fullpath's connectivity across DMS, CRM, website, and service lane produced more accurate propensity modeling than aM, which pulls only DMS data. Additionally, Fullpath's email templates drove higher open and conversion rates. CI noted this as a direct risk signal for similar vendors — including aM, which is a Cox Automotive product. Post-acquisition this competitive displacement dynamic is now internal.
AI Roadmap Method
🎙️
Fullpath Records All Client Performance Reviews for AI Roadmapping
CI documented that Fullpath records all performance review calls with dealers and feeds transcripts into an internal AI tool — generating consumer sentiment analysis, identifying product gaps, and dynamically updating the product roadmap based on what clients actually say in calls. This is a sophisticated, scalable voice-of-customer loop that most automotive vendors aren't running. It's a big reason their innovation pace is so high and their feature relevance is so strong.
Performance Model
👤
PMs Are Not a Differentiator — By Design
Clients do not view Fullpath's performance managers as a key value driver. The platform is intuitive enough that dealers customize settings and leverage AI recommendations without PM assistance. Fullpath's explicit intent is to automate as much human interaction as possible, reducing PMs to handling custom requests and support escalations only. This is a radically different model from Cox's consultant-heavy approach with MSS — and worth considering as you inherit the product presentation.
Inventory Expansion
🚗
vAuto Integration Already Being Used as IMS Wedge
CI noted Fullpath's existing inventory suite (aged inventory analysis, leads by vehicle, hot prospects, custom campaigns) and its integration with vAuto as a potential path toward dealers using Fullpath for traditional inventory management functions. CI assessment: Fullpath doesn't threaten to displace IMS vendors, but could make them more competitive if they integrate. An IMS vendor integrating with Fullpath gains its AI and unified data layer. Now that vAuto and Fullpath are both Cox products, this is a direct integration opportunity.
The So What for MSS Presentations
Presentation Strategy
The pre-acquisition CI threat narrative is now your value narrative
Every concern CI documented — consolidation play, CRM encroachment, aM displacement, vAuto wedge — has flipped. Those were threats when Fullpath was a competitor. As a Cox product, they become the reasons a dealer should consolidate onto the Cox ecosystem. The CI memos essentially wrote the competitive positioning you need: Fullpath's data layer is more connected than aM, faster than legacy CRMs, and already embedded in VinSolutions workflows. You're not selling a new tool — you're completing the ecosystem dealers already partially use.
🛡️
aM Displacement — Handle with Care
Live clients are already replacing automotiveMastermind with Fullpath. Since aM is also a Cox product, you'll need clear internal alignment on how to position both — avoid creating a situation where your own presentation accelerates aM churn before there's a coordinated strategy.
✅
Validate Enterprise Gap Status
Cross-rooftop record sharing was a documented gap as of late 2024. Before presenting to large dealer groups, confirm whether the Group CDP has closed this — the CI gap was specific enough that group-level buyers will ask.
🤝
PM Model Mismatch — Opportunity or Tension?
Fullpath is explicitly designed to minimize PM dependency. MSS is built around high-touch consulting. As you take over product presentation, think through whether the MSS consulting model adds genuine value on top of Fullpath's self-service intelligence — or whether you need a new engagement model for this product.
